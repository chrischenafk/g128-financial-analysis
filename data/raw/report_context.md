## RECURRING CALENDAR (month by month)

JANUARY
New Year's Day — Jan 1 (fixed). Light flag demand. "New Year" and decorative garden flags, fresh-start home décor.
Martin Luther King Jr. Day — 3rd Monday (floating). US flags, civil rights / commemorative flags. Half-staff awareness in some years.
Inauguration Day — Jan 20, every 4 years (next Jan 20, 2029). US flag and patriotic spike, political/party flags.
Lunar New Year prep. Chinese New Year usually late Jan–mid Feb. Supply-side, not demand: factories shut ~2–4 weeks. Your spring inventory must already be in transit.

FEBRUARY
Black History Month (all month). Pan-African flags, Juneteenth-adjacent designs.
Valentine's Day — Feb 14 (fixed). Heart / love-themed garden flags, seasonal home décor.
Presidents' Day — 3rd Monday (floating). Strong US flag spike, presidential and historical American flags. Big retail sale weekend.
Mardi Gras / Fat Tuesday (floating, Feb–early Mar). Mardi Gras décor flags, purple/green/gold.


MARCH
St. Patrick's Day — Mar 17 (fixed). Irish flags, shamrock garden flags, green décor. Reliable seller.
Spring transition (mid–late March). Garden flag décor season opens: floral, "welcome spring," Easter lead-in. This is the start of the décor engine's busy half-year.
Women's History Month (all month). Themed décor, suffrage flags.


APRIL
Easter (floating, Mar–Apr). Easter and spring garden flags, pastel/floral décor.
Tax Day vibe / spring cleaning. Home décor refresh, new-homeowner flags.

## One time Events
Past spikes worth learning from


Ukraine flags — Feb 2022 onward. Russia's invasion drove a massive, sudden solidarity spike in Ukrainian flags across Western markets. The textbook example of a geopolitical flag surge.
Pride/identity flag waves — recurring around legislation & court rulings. Major rulings or controversies spike specific identity flags.
Israel & Palestinian flags — Oct 2023 onward. The conflict drove sharp, sustained, and politically polarized demand for both flags. High marketplace-sensitivity: platforms apply content rules unevenly.
Afghanistan — Aug 2021. Withdrawal drove brief demand for Afghan national flags.
State flag redesigns. Minnesota and Utah adopted new state flags (effective 2024 / 2025). Redesigns create replacement demand and make old-design stock obsolete — watch for more states.
Royal / national mourning. E.g. Queen Elizabeth II's death (Sep 2022) drove UK flag and half-staff demand.
Confederate flag restrictions. Periodic marketplace and retailer bans removed SKUs overnight — a reminder that a single platform policy change can kill a product line.


Recurring geopolitical demand patterns
National independence days (each country's own date) spike that country's flag among its diaspora in the US. Examples: Mexico (Sep 16), India (Aug 15), many African and Caribbean nations through summer.
Elections worldwide spike political and national flags in the relevant communities.
Wars / invasions / solidarity movements — impossible to schedule, but keep raw flag stock or fast-turn sourcing for whichever country hits the news.


Weather-driven (semi-predictable)
Hurricane season (Jun–Nov, peak Aug–Oct). Storms destroy outdoor flags → replacement buying spikes afterward in affected regions.
Spring storm season. Same replacement dynamic.
